Operations Protocol

GM, 11.11.16

Before you start:

  • Deposit all loose jewelry, watches and anything on your person that might interfere with your work, on the desk.
  • Lab coats and goggles. Use your Fusor coat or a guest coat.
  • Nobody behind the machine except with a purpose. Electrical is exposed and dangerous.
  • Remove all unnecessary tools and other clutter from unit.
  • Check electrical plugs and electrical cables visually.

Valves:

  • Open two wall valves in Riser room (blue and red).
  • Open SEM pump valve at tank in Riser room (left side by wall.
  • Open both chiller pump valves at tank in Riser room (left side front).
  • Open two wall valves in SEM room (red and blue).

Cooling on (riser room):

  • Switch on SEM pump and chiller pump.
  • Switch on chiller

Startup:

  • Make sure SEM vac control switches are all down, except "Valve lock" which should be up.
  • Switch on both aux circuits (lower two switches on wall box).
  • Switch on SEM power 100V (under plastic cover).
  • Turn SEM key to main.
  • Turn SEM key to vacuum, hold for one second, release.
  • Follow "Manual Startup" sequence.

Shutdown:

  • Diffusion pump:
    • Close V1.
    • Switch off DP heater on vac panel.
    • Wait 15 minutes.
  • Close V2.
  • Close V4.
  • Turn off rotary pumps.
  • Turn off vac panel including "power". Every switch except "Valve lock" should be down.
  • Turn SEM key to "off"
  • Switch off SEM power 100V on wall box (under cover).
  • Switch off both aux circuits on wall box (lower switches).

Cooling off (riser room):

  • Switch off chiller.
  • Switch off SEM pump.
  • Wait 10 seconds.
  • Switch off chiller pump.

Close valves:

  • Close all valves at tank (Riser room).
  • Close wall valves (Riser room).
  • Close wall valves (SEM room).

Leaving:

  • Take your valuables from lock box.
  • Turn off lights in SEM room (Riser room is automatic).
  • Lock door.

Wires and detective work

GM, 2.27.16

Yesterday, LP, RS and GM connected all known (15 or so) cables between the units. We left one ground wire because we did not find a good spot for it, will do with more time.

Today, we looked at the orange controller on top and launched the hypothesis that it contains updated control electronics for the scope. It, and the black vacuum controller, were added significantly later than the initial purchase. We also know from the eBay picture that the orange control unit had the little gray sample amplifier sitting behind the specimen current module (all the way on the right as seen from the back). We are not sure what is connected to what there.

Hose work

LP, 4.15.16

We took the black hoses going to the pump array off and drained them; from the perspective of facing the hose nozzles, the hose on the left had oil in it, while the other had water. We then attached the two cut gray rubber hoses to the nozzles and tightened them with the hose clamps.
